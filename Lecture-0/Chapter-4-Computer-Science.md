## 📝 Handnotes

**Computer Science (CS)** is ultimately a powerful method of **problem-solving** — relating to both real-world and theoretical problems.

In this field, using a **computer** or learning to **program** means mastering a set of **tools and techniques** that help us find effective solutions to various practical problems.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

- ✅ CS is fundamentally the **science of problem-solving** — not just writing code
- ✅ Programming is a **tool**, not the goal itself
- ✅ Both **real-world and theoretical problems** fall within the scope of CS
- ✅ Learning CS means learning **how to think**, not just how to code
